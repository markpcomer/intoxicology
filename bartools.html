Your Starter Set of Home Mixology Bar Tools

The Wine Key

Wine Keys at Amazon

The first and last tool of any self-respecting home bartender. If your home bar is simply the beer and wine sort, then this is the only tool you'll need. Dinner soirees and raucous house parties intersect in once place and one place only: a dual-purpose wine and beer bottle opener.

The Jigger

Top-tier cocktails require carefully measured portions. Jiggers offer the quick and precise measurements necessary for easily elevated home cocktails. Speed-pouring is for rubes. This is the easiest entrance to an elevated cocktail game.

The Boston Shaker

Regardless of whether you want your martini shaken or stirred, this is the home bartender's apparatus for either. Boston shakers can make any non-bottled beverage. Also, shaking a drink is sexy.

The Hawthorne Strainer

Pouring unstrained shaken cocktails is so passé, like frosted tips, and Bill O'Reilly. Do yourself and your home guests a favor by straining each and every cocktail. Ice shards in an otherwise well-crafted, well-balanced cocktail is like driving a Jaguar with a "Live, Laugh, Love" bumper sticker.

The Bar Spoon

Listen, this is a crucial for a home bar tool kit. For stirred drinks, and liquid ingredient portions smaller than 1/4oz, this is a necessity. Also, discerning guests will be impressed by a bar spoon hiding among your spatulas, like a tiger hides in tall grass.

The Hand Juicer

Nothing, NOTHING beats the freshest juice possible in specific cocktails. This is THE difference between a good margarita and an phenomenal and memorable margarita. If there is a guest you really want to impress, squeeze some fresh juice in front of them for their desired cocktail.

Enjoy the phone number, thank me later.

Professional Grade Bar Tools for the Discerning Home Mixologist

The Mixing Glass

This is square one for Martinis, Manhattans, Old Fashioneds, and any cocktail that should be stirred. While 007 preferred his martinis shaken, not stirred, please know he possesses a licence to kill, not a cultivated palate.

Overly chilled cocktails numb your taste buds faster than you can say Bond, James Bond. So give yourself a licence to stir, and impress.

The Muddler

Any time you need to crush or squeeze ingredients, the muddler is the only tool for the job. If you want to give it a nickname, I highly recommend calling yours Bette Muddler.

I'll see myself out.

The Swiss Peeler

Outstanding cocktails require equally impressive garnishes. The Swiss Peeler is the best tool for twists. When your cocktail requires a cleanly cut, beautiful peel, this is the bar tool professionals reach for.

You wouldn't put cheap wrapping paper on an excellent gift, so make sure your cocktail's presentation matches its precision and quality.

The Paring Knife

Most mixed drinks and cocktails require citrus wheels or wedges. Dull knives produce damaged, ugly garnishes. I guess you could say the paring knife is example of "cutting-edge" cocktail technology.

Ice Trays

Many cocktails require specific ice cubes in order to maximize flavor. Bigger ice cubes melt more slowly than small cubes and pieces. Make sure your rocks glasses and highballs have the appropriate ice cubes and your cocktails will remain chilled and pure, instead of watered down and sad.

Speed Pours

Just because you have a jigger doesn't mean it's easy to accurately pour. Speed pours will greatly enhance the speed and precision of your pouring skills.

Also, speed pours make you look like you're a badass who knows what they're doing.

The Channel Knife

This is a simple and specific, yet highly distinguished tool. When you need long, thin strips of citrus peel, frankly there is no other tool. Case closed.

The Coco Strainer

This tool, when used in conjunction with the hawthorne strainer, produces the smoothest cocktails possible. The Coco Strainer filters out all of the smallest ice chards that the Hawthorne Strainer misses, so your cocktail will be the purest version of what it could be.

This is a tool of experts. You'll be a legend.

The Julep Strainer

Look, I've never made a Julep, but I know you can't make it without this. I use the Julep Strainer to scrape excess foam off of pints of beer and ale. So yeah, here we are.
